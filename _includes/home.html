My academic journey began with my first major in Software Engineering in 2006. After several years working as a programmer, I pursued my passion for language studies. I returned to academia, earning a BA in English Translation from Qazvin University and an MA in Linguistics from Allameh Tabataba'i University (Tehran) in 2015. The integration of my expertise in both fields led to my collaboration on interdisciplinary work in computational linguistics at Ruhr University Bochum in 2017, focusing on Biscuit Conditionals. I earned my Ph.D. in semantics-pragmatics from Konstanz University in 2024 with a dissertation titled "Bias Conditions in Polar Questions and Answers: A study of Bias Effects in Farsi". My visiting to UMass Amherst in fall semester 2023 enriched my academic experience. Now, I am a postdoc at Bielefeld University , collaborating on the CRC project.
